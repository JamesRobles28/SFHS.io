Accountancy, Business and Management(ABM)

Why ABM?, Heres why,

1. Complex Systems Analysis: ABM allows researchers to study complex systems by modeling the behaviors of individual
agents and observing the emergent properties of the system as a whole.

2. Bottom-Up Approach: ABM takes a bottom-up approach to modeling, focusing on the behavior
of individual agents rather than relying solely on aggregate-level data.

3. Flexibility: ABM is highly flexible and can be adapted to model a wide range of systems, from social networks and financial
markets to ecosystems and epidemiology.

4. Emergence and Self-Organization: ABM facilitates the study of emergent phenomena and self- organization within complex
systems, allowing researchers to explore how global patterns and structures arise from local interactions among agents.

5. Policy Analysis and Decision-Making: ABM can be used to simulate the effects of different policies and interventions within a system,
providing insights into their potential impacts and helping policymakers make more informed decisions.(etc.)

Humanities And Social Sciences(HUMSS)

Why HUMSS?, Heres why,

1. Broad Understanding of Society: HUMSS provides students with a comprehensive understanding of human behavior,
societies, cultures, and historical events.

2. Critical Thinking Skills: The strand emphasizes critical thinking, analysis, and interpretation of information.

3. Effective Communication: HUMSS focuses on written and oral communication skills, including writing essays,
giving presentations, and engaging in debates.

4. Preparation for College: HUMSS prepares students for college-level studies in humanities, social sciences, law,
communication, psychology, and related fields.

5. Understanding Diversity: Through the study of different cultures, societies, and historical periods, students gain
appreciation for diversity and learn to navigate a multicultural world with empathy and respect.

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics(STEM)

Why STEM?, Heres why,

1. High Demand Careers: STEM fields are in high demand across various industries, including technology,
healthcare, engineering, and finance.

2. Innovation and Creativity: STEM encourages innovation and creativity by fostering problem-solving skills and
encouraging students to think critically and analytically.

3. Interdisciplinary Approach: STEM integrates multiple disciplines, providing a holistic understanding of how science,
technology, engineering, and mathematics intersect.

4. Hands-On Learning: STEM education often emphasizes hands-on, experiential learning through lab experiments,
projects, and design challenges.

5. Global Relevance: STEM skills are globally relevant and transferable across borders,
making STEM graduates highly sought after in the international job market.

General Academic Strand(GAS)

Why GAS?, Heres why,

1. Broad Preparation: GAS provides a well-rounded education, covering a wide range of subjects across
different disciplines such as humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, mathematics, and languages.

2. Flexibility: GAS allows students to customize their curriculum based on their interests, strengths, and career goals.

3. College Readiness: GAS prepares students for higher education by developing critical thinking,
analytical, and communication skills essential for success in college-level courses.

4. Exploration of Interests: GAS encourages students to explore their interests and passions through a wide range of elective subjects.

5. Preparation for Entrance Exams: GAS equips students with the knowledge and skills needed to excel in college
entrance exams, such as the SAT, ACT, or university-specific admissions tests.
